A Quick Guide for

Indoor Plants

Explore

Indoor plants are popular because they are relatively easy to take care of, provide health benefits and can be used in a variety of indoor decor themes.

They're great options for those who have little to no yard space at their homes or for those that live in frigid climates with cold, long winters.

Caring for Your Plants

Here are some tips that will help you care for indoor plants:

-Keep potting soil moist- It’s important to make sure soil is not too wet nor too dry.

-Make sure the plant pot has drainage holes in the bottom of the pot.

-Place your plant near a light source, whether it’s natural or artificial.

Repotting

Unless your indoor plant has become too large for the pot it is in, you typically don’t need to re-pot indoor plants.

Watering

Over-watering is a common mistake, and you will want to make sure the plant actually requires water now or if it should wait until later.

Research

Do your research before buying a plant! It's as simple as that. Knowing the kind of plant and the expected conditions it should be living in will drastically improve the lifespan of it.

Just because a plant is large and sold for a low price does not mean you are getting the best deal. Doing some research prior to buying or speaking with a salesperson at a gardening center will help you out in the long run.

Sunlight

Succulents and cacti need continuous, daily sunlight. Plants with foliage need roughly 8 hours of light per day. The amount of light depends on the plants you are growing.

Soil

Keep potting soil moist. There are tools such as soil probes and moisture meters that will tell you the condition of your soil.

F.A.Q.

Which indoor plants are the easiest to take care of?

Sansevieria, philodendrons, most succulents, pothos, and ZZ plants are the easiest to take care of.

How do you prune indoor plants?

If you have no reason to prune plants, don’t do it. If you notice your plant getting tally and flimsy, then you can prune them.

How long do indoor plants typically live?

On average, indoor house plants last 2-5 years. After that, plants stop thriving and it’s best to invest in another plant.

Which indoor plants work well for small living spaces?

Philidrens, succulents, hedra helix, and devil's ivy.
